Welcome to Open Savannah!

The best way to dive into the Open Savannah community is to join us at our monthly Second Thursday night meetups. Here are a few things to do if you're new to our community.

  1. RSVP for Kickoff Event

    Don't miss our kickoff event Thurs. Feb. 23 at Bull Street Labs, where we'll be having a community brainstorming session, deciding on our first brigade civic tech project and doing a short collaborative exercise to build an app that helps residents find the contact information for their neighborhood association presidents. Go ahead and RSVP to save your spot

    Join Us and RSVP on Meetup

  2. Get set up with Slack

    We use slack to keep in touch when we aren't hanging out in person, which we share with techSAV. We also track ongoing conversations, research, and project development there. Once you're in Slack, make sure to join the `#opensavannah` channel, feel free to say hi in techSAV's #general channel and maybe tell everyone what makes you excited about civic tech or how you'd prefer to get involved.

    Join our Slack community

  3. Join Github (even if you don't code!)

    We use github to share and work on code-related projects. Many of our projects center around technology and therefore involve code, but you don't need to be able to write code to get involved! Github offers a range of ways to be part of the development process such as assisting in writing documentation or helping project managers keep track of work to be done.

    See our Github organization

  4. Follow along on Hackpad

    We use Hackpad for our bi-weekly updates. If you can't make it to our bi-monthly meetings, you can always stay informed by checking out the latest weekly update.

    Follow along on Hackpad
